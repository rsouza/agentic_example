Porto, 3 September 1888

Dear Mr. Harrington,

I received your letter of the 14th of August only last week, the post between Lisbon and Porto having been delayed by the harvest season. I am glad to hear that your research at the British Museum has yielded results and that you have located the manuscript you described.

As for the question you raised about Eça de Queirós, I can confirm that he was in Porto during the winter of 1878 and that he contributed several articles to the newspaper A Actualidade under a pen name. The editor at the time was a man called Rodrigues, whose family I know slightly. I will write to his widow in Braga and ask whether any correspondence with Queirós has survived.

The weather here has been mild this month, which is unusual. The river is lower than I have seen it in years. If you do visit Porto in October as you mentioned, the archive at the Torre do Tombo in Lisbon would also be worth a day of your time.

Yours faithfully,
Isabel Ferreira
